Guardian's Instructions

The Sacred Art of Bunny Observation

Dear Guardian,

This Watch Log is more than a mere journal—it is a chronicle of the magical relationship between observer and observed, guardian and guarded. Within these pages, you will document not just what you see, but the beginning of a deeper understanding between yourself and the forest realm.

How to Use Your Watch Log:

  1. Be Patient: The best observations come to those who wait. Find a comfortable spot, settle in, and allow the forest to forget you are there.
  2. Record Promptly: When a bunny graces you with its presence, note the details as soon as you can without disturbing the moment.
  3. Trust Your Instincts: If you sense a bunny has a name or personality, you are probably right. Guardians develop special insights.
  4. Look for Patterns: Recurring visitors often have routines. Note the time of day, weather conditions, and behaviors to understand these patterns.
  5. Respect Privacy: Observe from a distance that feels respectful. Never chase or disturb a bunny for a better view.

The Forest Guardian's Code of Ethics:

  • We do not chase. We only observe.
  • We always honor the bunny's choice to approach or retreat.
  • We protect the forest habitat with vigilance and care.
  • We share our knowledge only with those who will respect it.
  • We recognize that even in watching, we become part of the forest's story.

May your watch be peaceful and your observations plentiful.

— The Elder Guardians Council
